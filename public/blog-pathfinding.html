How to find your path in the community without getting overwhelmed

A plain-language guide for beginners and domain experts on choosing a role, a repo, and a first visible action.

Do not stop at reading

Choose one intent, one repo, and one small proof target this week. That is enough to start strong.

Simple pathfinding rule

If you are confused, do not try to understand the whole ecosystem at once. Start by answering three questions: what do I want now, what can I already contribute, and which repo helps me act this week?

  • If you want foundations, start with Intro to Computing and Programming.
  • If you want Python, AI, or automation, open HACKCLUB.
  • If you want web systems, open JAVASCRIPT or TYPESCRIPT.
  • If you want the wider ecosystem, stay in the Tech Community repo.

Good first actions

  • Join Discord and post your intent.
  • Give your email for repo-access follow-up.
  • Pick one project lane instead of watching everything.
  • Enter one sprint and leave one visible proof signal.

The goal is not perfect understanding. The goal is momentum.

Need help choosing? Use the assistant or the Start page and let the platform route you.
